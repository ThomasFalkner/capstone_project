Notes to the financial statements

1 — General Information

Pestana Berlin Ѕ.à r.l. ("the Company") was incorporated in Luxembourg on 8 October 2009 and is organized under the laws of Luxembourg as a "Société à responsabilité limitée" for a unlimited period.

The address of the registered office of the Company is 56, rue Charles Martel, L-2134 Luxembourg.

The principal object of the Company is the acquisition, sale, lease and/or management of movable property and real estate. The Company may alsoacquire. sell and hold investments in any companies. trademarks and licenses.

The Company manages and operates the Pestana Berlin Tiergarten Hotel located in Stülerstraße 6. 10787 Berlin, Germany.

The financial year of the Company starts on 1 January and ends on 31 December each year. The financial statements of the Company and respective notes are presented in Euros.

It is the opinion of the Board of Managers that these financial statements give a true and fair view of the operations of Pestana Berlin Ѕ.à r.l. as well as of its financial position and performance.

2— Summary of significant accounting policies

2.1 Basis of preparation

The annual accounts are prepared under the historical cost convention and in accordance with Luxembourg legal and regulatory requirements. Accounting policies and valuation rules are. in addition to those laid down by the Law. determined and applied by management.

2.2 Significant accounting policies

The main valuation rules applied by the Company are the following:

Tangible assets

Tangible assets are stated at cost less accumulated depreciation. Tangible assets are amortized over their estimated useful economic lives.

The cost of an item of plant and equipment comprises the purchase price of the asset. costs directly attributable to the acquisition and costs incurred in preparing the asset to be placed in its final position for being used. The financial costs incurred with loans obtained for construction of tangible assets are recognized as part of the construction cost of the asset.

Subsequent costs incurred with renovations and major repairs which result in increased lifetime or in the ability to generate further economic benefits will be considered in the future on the carrying amount of the asset.

The cost of repairs and maintenance of current nature will be considered in the future in the profit or loss as incurred.

The estimated useful lives of the most significant items of plant and equipment are as follows:

Live of goods
Plant and machinery 5 - 20
Other fixtures and fittings, tools and equipment 3

Whenever there is evidence of a loss of value of the tangible fixed assets. impairment tests are carried out in order to estimate the recoverable amount of the asset, and if necessary to register an impairment loss. The recoverable amount is determined as the higher of net selling price and value in use of the asset, being this last one calculated based on the discounted value of estimated future cash flows arising from the continued use and from the disposal of the asset at the end of its useful lives.

Useful lives of assets are reviewed at each financial reporting date, so that depréciation is charged in accordance with the consumption patterns of the assets. Changes to the useful lives, if any, are treated as a change in accounting estimate and are applied prospectively.

Current debtors and prepayments

Debtors are valued at their nominal value. They are subject to value adjustments where their recovery is compromised. These value adjustments are not continued if the reasons for which the value adjustments were made have ceased to apply.

Income tax

The income tax for the period comprises current tax and deferred tax. The income taxes are recorded in the income statement, except when they relate to items recognized directly in equity. The amount of current tax payable is determined based on profit before tax, adjusted in accordance with the applicable tax rules.

Deferred taxes are recognized using the liability method based on the statement of financial position, considering temporary differences resulting from the difference between the tax basis of assets and liabilities and their amounts in the financial statements.

Provisions

Provisions are recognized when the Company has: i) a present legal or constructive obligation resulting from past events, ii) to which is more probable than not that an outflow of economic benefits will be necessary to settle the obligation, and iii) the amount can be reasonably estimated. Whenever one of the criteria is not met or the existence of the obligation is conditional on the occurrence (or non-occurrence) of a certain future event, the Company discloses this fact as a contingent liability, unless the assessment of the economic benefits outflow is considered remote.

Income and expenses

Income and expenses are recorded in the period to which they relate, regardless of the payment or receipt.

In the hotel business, the revenue corresponds mainly to housing services. and sales related to the consumption of food and drinks in bars, restaurants and mini-bars, which are recorded on the date of consumption. For other services the revenue is recorded on the day of the service.

The revenue from operating lease contracts is recognised as revenue over the lease. No incentives are paid to renters at the beginning of the lease, so the revenue is not deducted from these amounts over the contract period.

Subsequent events

Events after the balance sheet date that provide additional information about conditions that existed at the balance sheet date (adjusting events) are reflected in the financial statements. Events after the balance sheet date that provide information on conditions occurring after the balance sheet date (non-adjusting events) are disclosed in the financial statements, if considered significant.

8 - Legal reserve

Luxembourg companies are required to appropriate to legal reserve a minimum of 5% of the annual net income for each financial year. This requirement is not applicable whenever there are retained losses and ceases to be necessary once the balance in the legal reserve reaches 10% of the issued share capital. The legal reserve is not available for distribution to shareholders before liquidation.
