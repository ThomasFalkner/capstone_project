REPORT OF THE STATUTORY AUDITOR

To the General Meeting of Infinigate Holding AG, Rotkreuz

Report of the Statutory Auditor on the Consolidated Financial Statements

As statutory auditor, we have audited the accompanying consolidated financial statements (pages 12 to 39) of Infinigate Holding AG, which comprise the consolidated balance sheet as at 31 March 2019, the consolidated income statement, the consolidated statement of changes in equity, the consolidated cash flow statement and notes to the consolidated financial statements for the year then ended.

Board of Directors' Responsibility

The Board of Directors is responsible for the preparation of these consolidated financial statements in accordance with Swiss GAAP FER and the requirements of Swiss law. This responsibility includes designing, implementing and maintaining an internal control system relevant to the preparation of consolidated financial statements that are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error. The Board of Directors is further responsible for selecting and applying appropriate accounting policies and making accounting estimates that are reasonable in the circumstances.

Auditor's Responsibility

Our responsibility is to express an opinion on these consolidated financial statements based on our audit. We conducted our audit in accordance with Swiss law and Swiss Auditing Standards. Those standards require that we plan and perform the audit to obtain reasonable assurance whether the consolidated financial statements are free from material misstatement.

An audit involves performing procedures to obtain audit evidence about the amounts and disclosures in the consolidated financial statements. The procedures selected depend on the auditor's judgment, including the assessment of the risks of material misstatement of the consolidated financial statements, whether due to fraud or error. In making those risk assessments, the auditor considers the internal control system relevant to the entity's preparation of the consolidated financial statements in order to design audit procedures that are appropriate in the circumstances, but not for the purpose of expressing an opinion on the effectiveness of the entity's internal control system. An audit also includes evaluating the appropriateness of accounting policies used and the reasonableness of accounting estimates made, as well as evaluating the overall presentation of the consolidated financial statements. We believe that the audit evidence we have obtained is sufficient and appropriate to provide a basis for our audit opinion.

Opinion

In our opinion, the consolidated financial statements for the year ended 31 March 2019 give a true and fair view of the financial position, the results of operations and the cash flows in accordance with Swiss GAAP FER and comply with Swiss law.

Emphasis of Matter

We draw attention to Note 4.15 to the consolidated financial statements which describes the uncertainty related to the outcome of the lawsuit filed against Infinigate Holding AG. Due to the uncertain outcome of the lawsuit, no provisions have been built in respect of a potential liability. Our opinion is not qualified in respect of this matter.

Report on Other Legal Requirements

We confirm that we meet the legal requirements on licensing according to the Auditor Oversight Act (AOA) and independence (article 728 Code of Obligations (CO)) and that there are no circumstances incompatible with our independence.

In accordance with article 728a para. 1 item 3 CO and Swiss Auditing Standard 890, we confirm that an internal control system exists, which has been designed for the preparation of consolidated financial statements according to the instructions of the Board of Directors.

We recommend that the consolidated financial statements submitted to you be approved.

1. Management Report

Infinigate Group

The Infinigate Group is one of the leading Value Added Distributors (VAD) for IT security solutions in Europe. At the end of the Fiscal Year 2018/19 (01.04.2018 - 31.03.2019), Infinigate employed a total of 375 people (previous year 289) in the group and generated sales of EUR 512.5 million (previous year 423.5 million). This corresponds to a revenue growth of 21.0% compared to the previous year.

We implemented a change in global accepted accounting policies during fiscal year 2018/19. The former consolidated financial statements have been prepared based on the accounting and reporting recommendations of the Swiss Code of Obligations (Articles 957 to 962). These financial statements have now been prepared based on the accounting and reporting recommendations of Swiss GAAP FER. The reason for the change in accounting policy is that it enables us to prepare our financial statements with a generally accepted accounting principle that is recognised by EU member states and allows us to file our group consolidated financial statements at the chamber of commerce of EU member states.

History

Founded in 1996, Infinigate has been active in the market since the start of the commercial Internet. Since 2002 Infinigate's sole focus has been the distribution of sustainable IT security solutions to protect and defend IT networks, servers, devices, data, applications as well as the cloud. In 2007 Infinigate acquired the former Nocom Security Group in Scandinavia with companies in Sweden, Norway and Denmark. In 2012 Infinigate entered the UK market with the acquisition of a majority stake in Vigil Software Ltd. and in 2014 followed the market entry in France with the establishment of Infinigate France SAS. In 2016 Infinigate acquired the remaining outstanding shares of Infinigate UK (former Vigil Software). In 2017 Infinigate Finland Oy was founded, H.I.G. Capital Europe invested in Infinigate to support the further geographical growth of Infinigate and Crypsys Data Security B.V. in the Netherlands was acquired. In November 2018 Infinigate acquired the acmeo Group, Germany's leading MSP/Cloud distributor.

Locations

Infinigate today operates in 11 European countries: Germany, England, France, Austria, Switzerland, Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland, Belgium and Netherlands. Infinigate Holding AG is headquartered in Rotkreuz (Switzerland).

Business Model

The modern world is characterized by an increasingly intertwined network-structure. In recent years, this trend has been reinforced by the increasing expansion of mobile networks. Information is accessible everywhere and at any time. The inclusion of networking into business processes is about to leap ahead, the "Internet of Things", "Industry 4.0" and "Cloud Computing" are increasingly becoming reality. These networking places ever increasing demands on the security and availability of the internal IT structures as well as the required data and information. The increasing inclusion of networking into business- and production processes not only enables faster and more accurate control, but also makes critical processes more vulnerable to third-party attacks (espionage, sabotage) or internal application errors. Functional IT security is thus becoming increasingly important. And this regardless of the current macroeconomic situation.

Already since 2002, Infinigate has specialized in the distribution of technologically leading security products for the protection of IT networks and sensitive electronic data. The focus is on the distribution of complex IT security solutions that protect corporate assets, provide secure external access, prevent attacks, protect sensitive information an the local and mobile networks, and the cloud from unauthorized access.

The needs of the market and trading partners form the basis for all activities. This applies both to the selection of the manufacturer's portfolio and to the design of the services offered to the partners. Infinigate constantly optimizes the manufacturer's portfolio in order to provide customers with the best possible solutions for their current IT security problems. The IT security products and services are distributed exclusively via a well-trained specialised channel (resellers, VARs, system and network integrators).

Success Factors

The success of Infinigate is based an several factors. Infinigate sees Value Add not only as a marketing technical term supplement, but aligns their entire action - for the benefit of manufacturers and partners. Thanks to these additional services, Infinigate is able to offer its manufacturers access to an international and high-qualified partner network.

The basis for the long-term market success is the training of employees and the consistent focus on service-oriented action.

Building on the know-how of its employees, Infinigate is able to sell more explanation-intensive products and supplement them with additional, sometimes fee-based services such as consulting, training and extended support, thus sustainably supporting their partners in the distribution of products.

In addition, Infinigate supports its partners, e.g. through the generation of leads, project acquisitions, as well as marketing activities such as trade fairs and roadshows, active in the further development of their business. This creates a dose customer loyalty, which also existed before the competition. Various awards from customers and manufacturers prove this.

Targets and Strategy

Infinigate intends to grow its business within the next 4 years to over EUR 1 billion, this by maintaining its strong focus on Cyber Security, sustainable growth of the organic business, further geographic expansion and the group wide rollout of the Cloud/MSP distribution model of the newly acquired acmeo GmbH.

By offering both on-premise and MSP Cyber Security Solutions Infinigate will remain and expand its attractiveness for both vendors, who seek to better penetrate the European market, and customers, who want to benefit from a strongly growing Cyber security market_ Additionally, the combination of on-premise and MSP solutions Infinigate is able to maintain strong operating cash flows while building up a stable and very scalable MSP business. The basic roll-out of the MSP distribution will take place in 2019 in dedicated countries, followed by the remaining group in 2020.

Acquisitions will remain playing a vital role in the further expansion of Infinigate. As in the past Infinigate will carefully weigh the benefits and cost of such acquisitions and only proceed with such acquisitions that are expected to deliver long term value to the group.

2. Economics

Economy and Industry

In 2018 the macroeconomic performance remained positive throughout the year, though not as dynamic as in 2017. The markets most relevant for Infinigate, IT and Cyber Security, grew faster than the overall economy. Infinigate was able to use the positive mometum to achieve a significant revenue increase and to gain further market share.

According to Gartner commercial spending an Cybersecurity will grow to $124 Billion in 2019. Total spending an information security products and services will reach more than $114 billion globally in 2018, an increase of 12.4% from last year, according to advisory company Gartner. Cyber attacks gain traction as fastest growing type of crime across the globe and particularly in the EU.

With the increase of devices used and an intensified attack rate the awareness for the need of thourough cyber security strategy will grow also in mid-size companies, the core market for Infinigate. From an end-customer segment point of view healthcare was the biggest growth area and will be during the next year, whereas financial institutions still hold the biggest overall market share. With the mid-size companies catching up an cyber security the manufacturing segment will grow in importance.

loT and BYOD

The emerging loT and BYOD trend among organizations has made devices and applications more exposed towards Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs). In addition, the increase in number of mobile devices within organizations makes it difficult for the IT teams to manage and treck the data flow in various systems. Therefore, organizations are adopting cyber security systems to protect the crucial information by monitoring, classifying, and resolving all kinds of attacks, which drives the market growth.

Cloud

Cloud computing model is widely adopted due to its powerful and flexible infrastructure option. Many organizations are moving toward the cloud solutions, to simplify the storage of data, as it provides remote server access an the Internet, which further allows access to unlimited computing power. Furthermore, the implementation of cloud-based model empowers organizations to manage all the applications, as there is no invisibility with exceptionally challenging analytics running in the background. In addition, the implementation of cloud can allow organizations to combine supplementary infrastructure technologies such as software-defined perimeters, to create robust and highly secure platforms.

Authentification

Organizations need strong authentication functionality to confirm that authentic employees access internal networks or specific applications. Enterprises are progressively moving towards multi-factor authentication to avoid the threats of password-based authentication. Multi-factor authentication is mostly performed by adding another element to the traditional user name and password. The layers can be hardware and / or software tokens, a device authentication step or a biometric check. The implementation of these authentication techniques to reduce the risk of cyber threats encourage users to adopt more thorough, effective cyber security solutions, thus providing a major opportunity for further market growth.

Infinigate as Relevant Market Player

Infinigate plays an important role in the market development and growth. With the upcoming new technologies there are still a lot of new vendors pushing into the market whilst the traditional market players often focus on leveraging the Channel infrastructure. With the acquisition of acmeo, a german based cloud and MSP Distributor, Infinigate accelerated the growth in the cloud and msp market. With eleven subsidiaries in Europe Infinigate can leverage the geographical expansion of the acmeo cloud and msp business model as well as attracting existing Infinigate vendors to move to this innovative sales channel.

Overall Infinigate was able to sign several new cyber security vendors and strengthen the portfolio significantly. This will accelerate into the next financial year and it is one of the priorities of the management to have a solid pipeline of new vendors as well as rolling out existing vendors throughout the whole group. As the industry matures in certain technologies the overall margin has been under pressure in certain segments and with certain vendors. Hence the onboarding of new vendors with new technologies and higher margins is one of the focus areas for the management team. In order to further tighten the relation to exisiting vendors and to better address potentially new vendors the position of a VP Vendor Management has been created.

3. Report in Forecast, Chances & Risks

Forecast

The cyber security market overall will still grow in the next couple of years by 8-10% annually. With the overall economy weakening in the European Union there might be an impact on many industries but lesser on the cyber security segment. With the increasing threat levels and awareness trickling down into the small and medium size business there will be still a solid growth opportunity. New technology will also drive the market with more sophisticated products also in the cloud and MSP area. The expectation is that the market will grow in Europe with a CAGR of approx. 9-10% in the next years.

As market leader in the value add cyber security distribution we have an excellent chance to participate in that growth and even outgrow the market further. Also the profitability will be healthy as long as there are new technologies and companies coming up, we detect them early enough and build the business with them. The significant vendor pipeline built up in 2018 shows already that we will be able to attract new and existing vendors for new territories by excellence in execution and focus.

In addition to technological approaches, we will continue to focus on expanding our commercial services for our partners. The increasing number of customers and transactions will be supported by improved processes in processing as well as the expansion of our online services such as the partner portal. The provision of financial services for project financing and support for our customers' growing foreign business (processing of exports) will also represent important competitive advantages in the future.

Well-trained and motivated employees are at the head of this business model. The efforts to make Infinigate an attractive employer also in the future will be further intensified.

Risks

The Board of Directors supervises the Executive Management and monitors its working methods by means of reporting and controlling mechanisms. At each meeting, the Board of Directors is informed by the CEO and CFO about the current course of business and important business transactions. At such meetings, the members of the Board of Directors may request from any other member of the Board of Directors or from the CEO or CFO any information about the Infinigate Group that they require to perform their duties. Extraordinary events are brought to the attention of the members of the Board of Directors without delay.

The Board of Directors of Infinigate Holding AG assesses corporate risks as part of systematic risk identification and assessment. On this basis, risk management measures are defined and implemented within the company.

Infinigate has established a clear system for managing risk that has been identified in a structured process. Infinigate (Infinigate Holding AG and its direct and indirect subsidiaries) hedge against these risks by implementing various safeguards and processes. In the area of customer relations, a calculable risk can be taken due to the existing economic situation.

Presentation of the main hedging strategies for the specific risks:

The international nature of our business leads to currency risks. The specific currency risk is assessed and a hedging strategy is defined. To manage this trisk we use forward rate agreements. Currently the hedging is done locally in alignment of local FX payment liabilities. In the course of the coming year a more centralised approach will be established to support the local entities, to better utilise natural hedge opportunities resulting from receivables and payables denominated in the same foreign currency and strengthen the group wide FX risk management.

Financial risks: We counter the risk of bad debt losses through standardized procedures, active receivables management and the partial use of credit insurance, leasing and guarantees. In addition to detailed local debtor management, we also monitor our most important customer relationships an the basis of personal assessments of business ratios.

Almost all business processes and workflows are IT-supported. A high level of security and a highly available design of the IT infrastructure with short recovery times are an essential part of our risk management.

Infinigate has established comprehensive employee retention programs.

Environmental and industry risks: Despite the stable economic situation, we are subject to risks in the area of consolidation of manufacturers and system houses. There is an existing trend where pan-regional players integrate local specialists. However, thanks to the high quality of our value-added services, dose fies to customers and manufacturers, and our broad market presence, we have so far been able to maintain the business within the new constellations that have arisen, and partly do even profit from them.

The organizational measures already taken in previous years to prevent the effects of negligent or deliberate misconduct by employees (compliance with data protection guidelines, IT legal structure, etc.) will be further expanded. Continuous training and close controlling ensure that risks are minimized. At the end of the fiscal year, there were no risks that could jeopardize the company's continued operation, nor are any such risks discernible in the future.

The company is involved in a lawsuit concerning the squeeze out of minority shareholders in May 2017. Some of the former minority shareholders have filed an action based on Art 105 FusG. The minority shareholders argue that the severance payment was inappropriate. Instead the plaintiffs claim a higher compensation. The company's legal advisors consider it unlikely that a court will rule fully in favor of the plaintiffs, while lt remains uncertain, if and to which extent the company will have to increase the severance payment going forward. The Board of Directors consider the paid severance payment as appropriate, however, a material uncertainty exists in this respect.

Chances

The opportunities for Infinigate arise as a specialized company in a rapidly changing market. Previous IT security concepts are increasingly reaching their limits and for the most part can no longer meet the massively increased requirements.

Developments such as the increase in CloudComputing in various facets, industry 4.0, Internet of Things and the increasingly demanded mobility in the working world, present IT security and network concepts with completely new challenges.

As the threat situation becomes more and more complex, companies and their IT service providers are constantly facing new challenges.

Infinigate is a valuable partner for many partners with its long-standing know-how advantage. The product and consulting know-how enables sales partners to react quickly to new customer requirements without having to map complete steps such as evaluation or Integration options in advance.

Due to the diversity of the requirements, end customers will increasingly resort to security services in the future. New technologies for attack protection, security automation and additional services for operational support must be made available to service providers at an early stage.

This opens up a variety of new business areas for Infinigate with its sales and service partner network. We are in the process of development and will present our solutions promptly.

In addition to technological approaches, we will continue to focus on expanding our commercial services for our partners. The increasing number of customers and transactions will be supported by improved processes in processing as well as the expansion of our online services such as the partner portal. The provision of financial services for project financing and support for our customers' growing foreign business (processing of exports) will also represent important competitive advantages in the future.

Well-trained and motivated employees are at the heart of this business model. We will intensify our efforts to ensure that Infinigate remains an attractive employer in the future.

Infinigate is not using any other financial instruments, nor does it enter into forward rate agreements that are not directly related to trade liabilities.

The Group companies include all companies that are directly or indirectly controlled by Infinigate Holding AG. In this respect, control is defined as the power to control the financial and operating activities of the respective company, so as to obtain benefits from its operations. Group companies are consolidated from the date on which control is transferred to the Group.

Companies acquired over the course of the year are consolidated in accordance with Group principles upon the date of acquisition. Goodwill from business combinations represents the amount of the acquisition costs which exceeds the proportional actual value of the net identifiable assets of the acquired company at the time of purchase. In the case of acquisitions, potentially existing non-capitalised intangible assets such as brands, user rights or client lists are not recognised separately, but remain in goodwill. Goodwill is amortised over a period of 20 years starting from the acquisition date.

All intragroup balances and liabilities as well as intragroup deliveries and services were offset and eliminated in the context of the consolidation. The interim profits were eliminated in the consolidation.

The financial statements of the companies included in the consolidated financial statements were prepared in accordance with uniform accounting and valuation principles.

2.7 Financial risk management - factors

The Group's financial risk management is essentially focused on identifying and analysing exchange rate risk, mainly against the US Dollar and the Euro, with the aim of minimizing its impact on Group EBTIDA. In order to hedge exchange rate risk, the Group may use derivative financial instruments such as forward currency contracts. Risk management is conducted by the local subsidiaries, who follow the directives issued by the Group's management bodies. Risks are assessed in collaboration with the local subsidiaries and the hedging methods are decided and implemented under the regular supervision of the Group's management body.

2.8 Sales and revenue recognition

Net sales include the inflow of economic benefits from the sale of goods and services within the scope of ordinary business during the period under review. Sales reductions, such as discounts, as well as payments to third parties such as commissions and any value added tax have been deducted from net sales reported. All intercompany sales are eliminated during consolidation.

Revenues are reported if a Group company has transferred the significant risks and rewards of ownership of products sold to the client, and the collectability of the related receivables is reasonably secured. Revenue from services is recognised in the accounting period in which the service is rendered. Accruals for discounts granted to clients are established in the same period as the sales that gave rise to the discounts under the terms of the contract. In the event of business transactions involving identifiable multiple elements, these are to be recognised and valued separately. This type of transaction occurs only rarely within the Group.

2.9 Cash and cash equivalents

Cash and cash equivalents in the balance sheet comprise petty cash, cash at banks and short-term deposits with an original maturity of three months or less. They are recorded at nominal value. In the cash flow statement, cash and cash equivalents consist of cash and cash equivalents as defined above.

2.10 Trade receivables from goods and services

Trade receivables are recognised and carried at the original net invoice amount less an allowance for any specifically impaired receivables. Impairment is charged on receivables, which are either more than 12 months overdue or for which specific risks have been identified. Bad debt are written off when there is objective evidence that the Group will not be able to collect the receivables. Allowance for impaired receivables as well as losses on trade receivables from goods and services are recognised as other operating expenses.

2.11 Prepayments

Includes payments on the assets side before the balance sheet date, insofar es they represent an expense for a specific period after that date.

On the liabilities side, prepayments before the reporting date are shown as deferred income insofar es they represent income for a specific period after this date.

2.12 Inventories

The inventories are recognised at the cost of acquisition in compliance with the strict minimum value principle. Inventories are subsequently valued at the lower of acquisition and fair value less cost to sell. Any discounts received are treated as cost reductions. Acquisition costs are determined according to the first-in-first-out (FIFO) method. Inventory with unsatisfactory turnover are revalued accordingly.

2.13 Property, plant and equipment

Property, plant and equipment are recorded in the balance sheet at historical cost less accumulated depreciation and any impairments. Acquisition costs comprise the purchase price as well as the costs directly attributable to the utilisation of the property, plant and equipment. Maintenance and repair costs that do not add value are charged directly to the result for the period.

Depreciation is calculated an a straight-line basis over the estimated useful life of the assets. The standardised useful life of property, plant and equipment ranges from two years for computer security equipment to eight years for office equipment, depending an the assets. Low-value assets are fully written off in the year of acquisition.

The residual values and the remaining useful life of property, plant and equipment are reviewed yearly and adjusted where necessary.

2.14 Intangible assets

Intangible assets are capitalised at acquisition or production.

Software and licenses

This heading included in particular the following positions:

- Licenses purchased granting rights to use new technologies and software. They are amortised over their useful life (maximum eight years)

- Internally devekoped software. These costs are recognised as intangible assets if it is likely that they will generate future economic benefits. The costs include software development employee costs.

The capitalised costs are amortised an a straight-line basis over the estimated useful life (maximum eight years).

2.15 Consolidated goodwill

Goodwill from business combinations represents the amount of the acquisition costs that exceed the proportional actual value of the net identifiable assets of the acquired company at the time of purchase. Goodwill from acquisitions is recognised at the time of acquisition.

Goodwill Amortisation

Goodwill is amortised over a period of 20 years starting from the acquisition date.

The Infinigate Group has been active in the European market for more than 20 years and has continuously expanded its dominant position as "Europe's most significant TRUE VAD with focus on Cyber Security and Cloud Business". The IT Security and Cyber Security market is a sustainably growing market with increasing importance for all businesses and organizations. Infinigate has established a relevant and sustainable position in this market.

As a Value Add Distributor, Infinigate relies on technology expertise and a knowledge space built up over decades, which is indispensable for vendors as well as partners. Infinigate has built and expanded decades of partner relationships based on its sustainable vendor offering and qualified solution selling. The partner relationships have existed for over 20 years and continue to develop through increased product penetration and sustainable growth.

The newly acquired but also sustainably developed MSP & Cloud business complements and reinforces these characteristics: (a) continuous growth, (b) no customer churn, (c) high value offering with sustainable strong margin above market average, (d) very high recurring revenue share.

As a result of the scaling of the MSP & Cloud business across the entire European Infinigate organization and all vendors, Infinigate's sustainable market position as the dominant MSP distribution expert will also increase.

Against the background of these aspects, the picture is strengthened that the goodwill built up over the years as a result of successful M & A activities represents a sustainable value that justifies a linear depreciation of 20 years.

Impairment

Infinigate reviews, at least once annually, the recoverable amount of the goodwill capitalised. This requires an assessment of the value in use of the subsidiaries defined as underlying cash-generating units. The estimated factors such as volume, selling prices, revenue growth, gross profit margin, operating expenses and investments, market conditions, balance sheet structure and other economic factors, as well as parameters derived from external data (e.g., discount rates) are based on assumptions that management considers appropriate.

Tax provisions and other provisions are formed for all uncertain liabilities and anticipated losses from pending transactions. 2.19 Financial liabilities

Financial debts are recognised as nominal values. Any transaction costs incurred are posted directly in the income statement. Financial debts are shown on the balance sheet as non-current liabilities, unless the Group has an obligation to settle the debt within at least 12 months after the balance sheet date.

2.20 Income taxes

The tax expense for the period comprises current income taxes and deferred taxes. Tax is recognised in the income statement.

Current income tax

Current tax liabilities and assets for the current period and Prior period are measured at the amount expected to be paid to or recovered from the taxation authorities. The tax rates and tax laws used to compute the amounts are those that are enacted or substantively enacted at the balance sheet date.

Deferred tax

Deferred tax is recognised in full, using the balance sheet approach, on temporary differences between the tax bases of assets and liabilities and their carrying amounts in the financial statements.

Deferred tax is determined using tax regulations and rates that have been enacted or substantively enacted at the balance sheet date and are expected to apply.

Deferred tax assets and liabilities are recognised for all temporary differences and are offset if a legally enforceable right exists to offset current tax assets against current tax liabilities and the deferred taxes relate to the same taxable entity and the same taxation authority.

2.21 Pension benefit obligations

The Group operates the pension plans in accordance with the legal requirements. Their assets are generally held in collective foundation pension funds. The pension plans are funded by employee and employer contributions.

Pension plans are dealt in accordance with Swiss GAAP FER 16.

Any actual economic impacts of pension plans on the company are calculated as at the balance sheet date. An economic benefit from a surplus is capitalized provided this is admissible and the surplus is to be used to decrease the company's future contributions to its pension plans. An economic liability is recognized if the conditions for forming a provision are met. Contributions to to the pension plans are recognized in the income statement in the year they are made.

The Group is involved in a lawsuit concerning the squeeze out of minority shareholders in May 2017. Some of the former minority shareholders have filed an action based an Art. 105 FusG. The minority shareholders argue that the severance payment of EUR 7.5 million (equal to a value of EUR 142.27 per share) was inappropriate. Instead, the plaintiffs claim a compensation of at least EUR 402.27 per share.

The Group's legal advisors consider it unlikely that a court will rule fully in favor of the plaintiffs, while it remains uncertain, if and to which extent the Group will have to increase the severance payment going forward. The Board of Directors consider the paid severance payment as appropriate; however, a material uncertainty exists in this respect.

As per the statutory cost regulation, pursuant to Art. 105 FusG, the Group must bear the legal costs for determining the appropriateness of compensation for minority shareholders, which are expected within the range of EUR 637 thousand for the first-instance litigation. In exceptional cases, i.e., if the applicants' litigation appears forlorn, the court may impose the litigation costs to be born by the plaintiffs, which Infinigate Holding AG has asserted in the current process. Because there is at least an increased risk that the court will apply the statutory cost regulation, the Group has recognized an amount of EUR 637 thousand as a provision in FY 2017/18. During FY2018/19, an amount of EUR 200 thousand has already been used to cover incurred litigation costs.
