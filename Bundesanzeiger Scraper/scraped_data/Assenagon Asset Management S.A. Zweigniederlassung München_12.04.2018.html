Notes to the annual accounts for the financial year ended 31 December 2016

1. General information

ASSENAGON ASSET MANAGEMENT S.A. (hereafter « the Company ») was incorporated on 3 July 2007 for an unlimited period under the legal form of a Public limited company under the laws of Luxembourg.

The purpose of the Company is the management of luxembourgish and foreign UCITS funds, UCI funds and specialised investment funds, as well as the management of alternative investment funds (AlF), which are in accordance with Annex I of the Luxembourg Law of 12 July 2013 on alternative Investment fund managers (the "Law of 12 July 2013") and the marketing and sales of units or shares of UCITS funds and/or UCI Funds and/or AlF in Luxembourg or abroad.

The Company is licensed as Management Company under the provisions of Chapter 15 of the Law of 17 December 2010 relating to undertakings for collective investment and the Law of 12 July 2013. In addition, the Company is subject to the Law of 10 August 1915 on commercial companies, as amended.

The registered office of the Company is established in 1B, Heienhaff, L- 1736 Senningerberg. The Company is registered under number B 129.914 at the Luxembourg Register of Commerce.

The Company's financial year starts an January 1st and ends on December 31st of each year.

In 2008, the company incorporated a branch in Munich. In March 2015, the branch opened an office in Frankfurt/Main.

The Company is a 100% subsidiary of Assenagon S.A., Luxembourg.

The Company falls under the criteria for "medium-size" companies in the sense of the law of 19 December 2002 an the Register of Commerce and Companies and the accounting and annual accounts of undertakings as amended.

2. Summary of significant accounting policies

2.1. Basis of preparation

The annual accounts have been prepared under the historical cost convention and under the going concern assumption in accordance with Luxembourg legal and regulatory requirements.

Accounting policies and valuation rules are, besides the ones laid down by the law of 19 December 2002 (as amended) determined and applied by the Board of Directors.

The preparation of annual accounts requires the use of certain accounting estimates. lt also requires the Board of Directors to exercise its judgment in the process of applying the accounting policies. Changes in assumptions may have a significant Impact on the annual accounts in the period in which the assumptions changed. The Board of Directors believes that the underlying assumptions are appropriate and that the annual accounts therefore present the financial position and results fairly.

The Company's books are kept in Euro (EUR).

The Law of 18 December 2015, amending the Law of 19 December 2002 on the Register of Commerce and Companies and the accounting and annual accounts of undertakings, and the Grand-Ducal Regulation as of the same date, have revised the layout of the balance sheet and profit and loss account.

To apply these new provisions, the allocation of accounts and the presentation of the annual accounts as of 31 December 2016 have been modified. In order to ensure comparability, the previous year's figures have been reclassified in the same way, without any Impact on the result for the financial year 2015.

2.2. Significant valuation rules

The main valuation rules applied by the Company are the following.

2.2.1. Intangible assets

Intangible assets made up of licences are valued at purchase price including the expenses incidental thereto or at production cost; less cumulated depreciation amounts written off and value adjustments. These value adjustments are not continued, if the reason for which the value adjustments were made have ceased to exist. Intangible fixed assets are depreciated on a linear basis over 3 years.

2.2.2. Tangible assets

Tangible assets are valued at cost less accumulated value adjustments. Value adjustments are made on a linear basis over the useful lives of the assets.

The following depreciation rates have been applied:

Plant and machinery: 25 to 33.33%
Other fixtures, fittings and equipment: 25%
IT (hardware): 33.33%

2.2.3. Debtors

Debtors are valued at their nominal value. They are subject to value adjustments, where their recovery is compromised. These value adjustments are not continued, if the reasons for which the value adjustments were made have ceased to apply.

2.2.4. Prepayments

This balance sheet position includes expenses booked before the balance sheet date relating to a subsequent financial year.

2.2.5. Provisions

Provisions are intended to cover losses or debts, the nature of which is clearly defined and which, at the date of the balance sheet, are either likely to be incurred or certain to be incurred but uncertain as to their amount or the date which they will arise.

Provisions may also be created to cover charges that have originated in the financial year under review or in a previous financial year, the nature of which is clearly defined and which, at the date of the balance sheet, are either likely to be incurred or certain to be incurred but uncertain as to their amount or the date on which they will arise.

2.2.6. Creditors

Creditors are recorded at their nominal value or at their reimbursement value.

2.2.7. Foreign currency translation

The company maintains its books and records in Euro (EUR).

Transactions expressed in currencies other than EUR are translated into EUR at the exchange rate effective at the time of the transaction. Formation expenses and long term assets and liabilities expressed in currencies other than EUR are translated into EUR at the time of the transaction. At the balance sheet date, these assets remain translated at historical exchange rates.

Cash at banks is translated at the exchange rate effective at the balance sheet date. Exchange losses and gains are recorded in the profit and loss account of the year.

Other assets and liabilities are translated separately respectively at the lower or at the higher of the value converted at the historical exchange rate or the value determined on the basis of the exchange rates effective at the balance sheet date. Solely the unrealised exchange losses are recorded in the profit and loss account. The exchange gains are recorded in the profit and loss account at the moment of their realisation.

Trade debtors mainly include management- and performance fees for the Funds managed by the Company. Other debtors include mainly management fees for Funds where the Company is investment manager.

5. Prepayments

Prepayments are mainly made up of prepaid expenses for IT maintenance.

6. Capital and reserves

6.1. Subscribed capital

As at 31 December 2008, the subscribed capital amounted two million Euro (EUR 2,000,000.00) divided into twohundredthousand (200,000) fully paid up voting shares with a nominal value of EUR 10 each. During the year 2009, the subscribed capital was increased to three million Euros (EUR 3,000,000.00), divided into threehundredthousand (300,000) voting shares having a nominal value of ten Euros (EUR 10) each. By decision of the Board of Directors of 30 March 2009 a loan granted by Assenagon S.A. was converted into share capital.

As at 31 December 2016, the subscribed capital amounts to five million Euros (EUR 5,000,000.00) divided into five hundred thousand voting shares of ten Euros (EUR 10) each, all fully paid-in.

6.2. Legal reserve

The Company is required to allocate a minimum of 5% of its annual net income to a legal reserve, until this reserve equals 10% of the subscribed capital. This reserve may not be distributed.

6.3. Other reserves

As at 31 December 2016, the Company reduced its Net Wealth Tax liability in accordance with paragraph 8a of the Luxembourg Net Wealth Tax Law. The Company allocates under non-distributable reserves an amount that corresponds to five times the amount of reduction of the Net Wealth Tax. This reserve is non-distributable for a period of five years from the year following the one during which the Net Wealth Tax was reduced.

As at 31 December 2016, the reserve for net wealth tax was made up of a reserve made for the year 2012.
