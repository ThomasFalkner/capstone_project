Amundi has been part of the Amundi Group tax consolidation grouping since 1 January 2010.

EMPLOYEE PROFIT-SHARING AND INCENTIVE PLANS

Some Group companies form an Economic and Social Unit ("UES"). This employee profit-sharing is recognised in the income statement in the year in which the employees' rights are earned. Its cost is included in Employee expenses.

An estimated charge corresponding to incentive plans is recognised in the income statement in the same way as employee profit-sharing. Its cost is included in Employee expenses. At 31 December 2015, incentive plans represented €k 6,434.

Employees assigned ("seconded") by Crédit Agricole SA operate under agreements signed as part of the relevant entity's UES. The estimated expense to be paid for profit-sharing and incentive plans has been recognised in the financial statements.

ACCOUNTING PRINCIPLES AND VALUATION METHODS

PRESENTATION OF THE FINANCIAL STATEMENTS

The information pertaining to the 2015 fiscal year provided in the financial statements and notes is stated in thousands of euros.

GENERAL PRINCIPLES

The financial statements of Amundi AM were prepared in accordance with French generally-accepted accounting principles (the Plan Comptable Général, Art. 531-1) and the rules established by Crédit Agricole S.A. as a Central Body tasked with adapting these generally-accepted principles to the specific characteristics of Crédit Agricole Group.

CHANGES TO ACCOUNTING METHODS AND THE PRESENTATION OF THE FINANCIAL STATEMENTS

No changes to accounting methods or to the presentation of the financial statements have been applied since the previous reporting period.

MARKETABLE SECURITIES

These securities are recorded at their purchase price, excluding fees. Realised gains and losses are determined using the first-in, first-out method. Any losses in relation to the probable trading value, determined line by line, are assigned a provision for impairment.

Short-term investment securities in foreign currencies are recognised at their acquisition cost in currency and translated into euros on the closing date.

EQUITY INVESTMENTS AND SUBSIDIARIES

Equity investments and subsidiaries are recognised at historical cost. Realised gains and losses are determined using the first-in, first-out method. At end of the reporting period, they are individually assigned impairment provisions when their value in use is lower than the historical cost.

Information on revenue and income for the current reporting period is subject to their approval by the annual general meetings.

FOREIGN CURRENCY TRANSACTIONS

Amounts receivable or payable in foreign currencies are translated into euros using the exchange rate in force on the closing date, giving rise to a translation adjustment, recorded as an asset when the difference corresponds to an unrealised loss and a liability in case of an unrealised gain. These accrual accounts are not included in income, but a provision for exchange rate risk is recorded for unrealised translation adjustment losses.

BRANCHES

The branches produce their own financial statements. Their accounts are aggregated with the equivalent head office accounts.

They are translated into euros using the following method:

―

Balance sheet items with the exception of liaison accounts corresponding to investments and income are translated using the year-end exchange rate.

―

Liaison accounts corresponding to investments are translated at the historic rate.

―

Income and expense accounts are translated at the historic rate.

Differences arising from the application of historic rates and the closing exchange rate are recorded under retained earnings as translation differences.

FIXED ASSETS

Fixed assets appear on the balance sheet at their purchase price. They are depreciated over their estimated useful lives, i.e.

Fixtures and fittings 5 to 10 years, straight-line method
Computer equipment 3 years, straight-line method
Office equipment 5 years, straight-line method

Software recognised as intangible assets is amortised over 3 years using the straight-line method.

Business assets, recognised in the amount of €k 99,246, were subject to depreciation of €k 1,067.

POST-EMPLOYMENT BENEFITS - RETIREMENT PLANS - DEFINED-CONTRIBUTION PLANS

Employers contribute to a variety of compulsory pension schemes. Plan assets are managed by independent organisations and the contributing companies have no legal or implied obligation to pay additional contributions if the funds do not have sufficient assets to cover all benefits corresponding to services rendered by employees during the year and during prior years.

Consequently, Amundi AM has no liabilities in this respect, other than contributions to be paid for the most recent financial year.

The amount contributed for these retirement plans is recorded under "Employee expenses".

RETIREMENT, EARLY RETIREMENT AND END-OF-CAREER ALLOWANCE COMMITMENTS -DEFINED-BENEFIT PLANS

Amundi AM applies Conseil National de la Comptabilité Recommendation 2003-R.01 relating to the measurement and recognition of retirement and similar benefit obligations.

In accordance with this regulation, Amundi AM sets aside provisions to cover its retirement and similar benefit obligations falling within the category of defined-benefit plans.

The rate applied at 31 December 2015 was 2.03%, and the commitment amounted to €k 31,332.

At 31 December 2015, the sensitivity analysis showed that:

―

a 50 basis point increase in discount rates would reduce the commitment by -7.56%,

―

a 50 basis point decrease in discount rates would increase the commitment by 8.39%.

Within Amundi Group, Amundi AM has entered into an insurance contract with PREDICA to cover end-of-career allowances (IFC) and mandates have been signed between Amundi AM and the UES subsidiaries.

INCOME AND EXPENSES

Fee and commission income and expenses are recognised in income based on the nature of services with which they are associated.

Interest and fee and commission income considered as interest is recognised in the income statement on a pro rata basis.

Fees and commissions in consideration of non-recurring services are immediately recognised in the income statement.

Fees and commissions in consideration of ongoing services are recognised over the duration of the service rendered.

EXECUTIVE COMPENSATION

The arrangements for executive compensation are known to the sole shareholder.

CORPORATE TAX

The tax charge appearing in the income statement is the income tax due for the reporting period. It includes the exceptional 10.7% contribution and the 3.3% social security contribution.

PARENT COMPANY

Amundi AM is consolidated by Amundi and by Crédit Agricole SA.

OWNERSHIP STRUCTURE

The share capital of Amundi AM is composed exclusively of fully paid-up ordinary shares with a nominal value of €15 each.

The number of ordinary shares at 31 December 2015 is 39,750,841; Amundi holds 39,750,836 shares. The companies Amundi Immobilier, Etoile Gestion, Société Générale Gestion, Valinter 19 and BFT Investment Managers each hold one share.

STATUTORY AUDITORS' FEES

The company is fully consolidated in Amundi's financial statements. As a result, information relating to statutory auditors' fees is indicated in the notes to the consolidated financial statements of Amundi Group.
